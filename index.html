Applied Partial Differential Equations Seminar

Welcome to the University Cincinnati Applied Partial Differential Equations (PDE) Seminar website. The seminar focuses on the analytical and computational methods to study solutions of PDE and their applications in various fields.

The Applied PDE Seminar runs biweekly and the talks are offered in an online format. The Zoom links can be found under each talk’s announcement. You can find the extended abstract of a talk by clicking on the title of the talk. All seminars are on Tuesdays at 4:00 pm Eastern Time.

For more information, please contact Deniz Bilman.

Mathew Johnson
Nov 9, 2021 4:00 PM Online (Zoom)

Stability of periodic Lugiato-Lefever Waves

In this talk I will describe recent advances in the stability analysis of $T$-periodic stationary solutions of the Lugiato-Lefever equation, a damped nonlinear Schrödinger type equation with forcing that arises in nonlinear optics. Several recent works have studied the stability of such waves to so-called ‘subharmonic’ perturbations, i.e. $NT$-periodic perturbations for some natural number $N$. However, these results are degenerate for large $N$ since both the rate of decay of perturbations, along with the domain of attraction, both tend to zero as N tends to infinity. In this talk, I will discuss a new methodology for obtaining subharmonic stability results for the LLE which are uniform in $N$. This approach is quite general, and applies to a wide class of dissipative models.
